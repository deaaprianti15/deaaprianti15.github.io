c). Adjective Orders (Adjective of quality)

The third Point in this chapter is the adjective of quality. Adjective of quality is an adjective that describes or describes the quality of a noun or pronoun such as person/people (people), animals (animals), and things (objects).

Here are the types of adjectives / adjectives that indicate a quality:

  1. Age : ancient (kuno/purba), medieval (pertengahan), new (baru), old (tua), etc.
  2. Colors : yellowo (kuning), red (merah), purple (ungu), green (hijau), blue (biru), etc.
  3. Feelings : happy (bahagia), angry (marah), glad (senang), upset (gundah), etc.
  4. Materials : alumunium, cotton, silver, sythetic, etc.
  5. Opinions : handsome (tampan), pretty (cantik), hot (panas), etc.
  6. Origin : Indonesian, French, latin, etc.
  7. Qualities : beautiful, good, nice, etc.
  8. Shapes : round, rectangular, square, etc.
  9. Sizes : large, small, thin, etc.
  10. Time : daily, weekly, monthly, etc.

Example of sentences using adjectives

  • The cat is adorable
  • The rabbit is white like snow and she's very cute
  • The weather is nice
  • The park is shady
  • I am very angry
  • Halimah is a good student
  • Edo is very helpful
  • Lina is very happy
  • Your room is very unique. It has two pink chairs, a rectangle table, and a blue bookshelf

Usually we often add some adjectives in front of nouns, but to indicate the Opinion, adjectives are placed in the front most urutang, for example:

  • A handsome young man
  • A nice white shoe

Adjectives have order rules in English which are as follows:

Opinion — size — shape — age — colour — nationality/origin — material — purpose

Examples:

  • My young sister has beautiful long brown hair
  • Yesterday, my family and I visited a lovely little old village

Ketika ada dua atau lebih kata sifat yang berasal dari group yang sama, maka harus di tambah -and

  • The zebras has black and white stripes on their bodies
  • Edo is very helpful and kind
  • I will keep my house clean and tidy

When there are three or more adjectives that belong to the same group, you can add a comma (,) between the adjectives.

Examples:

  • He lost a grey, white, and black limited-edition watch
  • My beloved friend lives in the small brown, black, and dark blue house at the end of the street
  • She is very kind, polite, and clever girl
